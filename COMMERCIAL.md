For almost everyone reading this, the AGPL costs nothing and asks nothing. If
you self-host Finlynq for yourself, your family, or your own organization's
internal use, you are done. Use it, modify it, keep the license notice intact.

## When the AGPL becomes a problem

Section 13 of the AGPL is the "network clause". If you modify Finlynq and make
that modified version available to users over a network, you must offer those
users the complete corresponding source of your modified version, under the
AGPL.

The obligation is triggered by *offering the service*, not by shipping software.
So it reaches you if both of these are true:

- you run a modified Finlynq as a hosted product for other people, or embed it
  into a larger product or service you offer over a network, **and**
- you cannot or will not publish the full source of what you built on top of it.

If you are willing to publish your source under the AGPL, you need nothing from
us. That is the bargain the license offers, and it is open to everybody on
identical terms.

## The alternative

A commercial license is available that lifts the copyleft obligations, so you
can build on Finlynq without releasing your own source. Same codebase, different
terms.

Write to **legal@finlynq.com** with a short description of what you are
building, and we will send you terms.

## What this is not

This is not open core. There is no crippled community edition and no features
withheld from this repository. The managed service at
[finlynq.com/cloud](https://finlynq.com/cloud) runs the same code you are
looking at. The commercial license exists to give companies a way out of
copyleft, not to gate functionality.
